Based on ~72kg targeting ~65kg. Protein held constant to preserve muscle. Carbs lower on rest days. As weekly volume exceeds 50km, nudge training day carbs to 290–310g. Hit protein first, carbs second, let fat fill the rest.
1
Protein first. 150g daily no matter what. Spread across meals — 35–40g per sitting. Most important number on the sheet.
2
Carb timing. Eat most carbs around training. Large carb meal 2–3 hours before quality sessions or long runs. Good carb meal within 60 min of finishing any run.
3
Don't undereat on big days. Long runs and quality sessions require more food, not less. Under-fuelling raises injury risk and ruins the next session. Weight loss happens on rest days.
4
Recovery window. Within 30–60 min of finishing any run: protein + carbs. Protein shake and banana, Greek yoghurt with fruit, or eggs on toast.
5
Hydration. 2–3 litres daily. Dark urine = drink more. Electrolytes on any run over 60 minutes.
6
Alcohol. One or two drinks won't matter. More than that on a training night will hit sleep quality and compromise the next session.
7
Consistency over perfection. One poor day is nothing. A week of poor eating matters. Aim for 80–90% adherence across the block.
8
Weight loss pace. Max 0.5kg/week. Faster and you're losing muscle. Volume does most of the work — don't force it.
Night Before
Carbohydrate-rich dinner. Pasta, rice, or potatoes with protein. Nothing rich, spicy, or unfamiliar. Eat at a normal time.
Morning Of
Eat 2–3 hours before. Oats, toast, or rice with moderate protein. Low fat and fibre to aid digestion. If eating close to run time, keep it small.
60–90 min+ runs
Start fuelling at 45 minutes — don't wait for hunger. Aim for 40–60g carbohydrates per hour. Gels, chews, dates, banana. Test options in training — never try something new on race day.
3hr+ runs
60–80g carbohydrates per hour. Electrolytes every 60–90 minutes. Sip water regularly rather than large amounts infrequently.
After
Within 30 min: protein + carbs. Rehydrate — weigh before and after long runs to gauge sweat loss. Proper meal within 2 hours.
What to Carry
90min+: vest or belt, 500ml–1.5L water, 2–4 gels, electrolyte tabs. 3hr+: 1.5L+ water, 6–8 gels or real food, salt tabs, spare snack.
Race Week
Increase carbs from Wednesday — extra serving of rice, pasta, or oats per meal. Keep protein constant. Stay hydrated throughout the week.
Race Eve
Familiar carb-rich dinner, eaten early (6–7pm). Nothing new. Prepare kit, drop bags, and nutrition the evening before so race morning is calm.
Race Morning
2–3 hours before start. 80–100g carbohydrates, moderate protein, low fat and fibre. Porridge, toast, or rice. Sip water but don't overdrink. Coffee is fine if that's your routine — don't change it.
First 10 Miles
Start fuelling at 30–40 minutes even if fine. The first 10 miles must feel easy. Eat to a schedule, not hunger. Walk all significant climbs.
Aid Stations
Use every one. Top up water and take food even if not hungry. Real food becomes more appealing than gels in the second half.
Drop Bags
Pack more than you need. Something salty, something sweet, spare gels. Dry top if weather is variable. Blister kit.
Miles 30+
If stomach turns, switch to salty real food — soup, broth, flat coke. Keep moving. Walk if needed but don't stop.
